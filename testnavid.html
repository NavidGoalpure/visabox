Australian Skills Assessment Authority: VETASSESS

Australian Skills Assessment Authority, VETASSESS, assesses and evaluates a wide range of professional fields. VETASSESS evaluates occupations related to sports, marketing, agriculture, management, as well as fields like microbiology, biochemistry, and many others. With VETASSESS, you do not need a language score to start the process. If you search for the term VETASSESS OCCUPATION LIST on Google or visit this link, you can see the conditions and requirements for each occupation assessed by VETASSESS. The organization classifies occupations into five groups based on their required educational level.

Group A

This group includes occupations where the applicant, for a positive assessment, must have an equivalent Australian Bachelor's degree or higher, and the educational qualification and work experience of the applicant must be directly relevant. Additionally, the applicant must have at least one year of relevant work experience in the last five years related to the occupation and their university degree.

Group B

This group includes occupations that require an equivalent Australian Bachelor's degree or higher. This group is further divided into three categories:

  1. If the educational qualification and work experience of the applicant are fully relevant, the applicant must have at least one year of work experience within the last five years since obtaining the degree.
  2. If the educational qualification and work experience of the applicant are semi-relevant, and the applicant holds a diploma equivalent to an Australian diploma that is fully related to the occupation, the applicant must have at least two years of work experience within the last five years since obtaining the diploma.
  3. If the educational qualification and work experience of the applicant are unrelated, the applicant must have at least three years of work experience within the last five years since obtaining the degree.

Calculation of Work Experience Before Obtaining the Degree: If all the work experience of the applicant is before obtaining the bachelor's degree, a total of 5 years of work experience related to the required degree is required. The applicant must also have worked for at least one year after obtaining the bachelor's degree.

Group C

This category includes jobs that require at least an AQF Diploma, and the applicant must have a minimum of one year of work experience after obtaining the diploma in the past five years. For this group of occupations, there are three scenarios based on the relationship between the job and the university degree:

  1. If the applicant's educational qualification and work experience are directly related, they must have a minimum of one year of work experience within the past five years after obtaining the diploma.
  2. If the applicant has another qualification equivalent to AQF Certificate IV in a closely related field, they must have a minimum of one year of work experience within the past five years after obtaining the diploma.
  3. If the applicant's educational qualification and work experience are unrelated, they must have a minimum of two years of work experience within the past five years after obtaining the diploma.

Calculation of Pre-Degree Work Experience: If all of the applicant's work experience is before obtaining the degree, a minimum of 5 years of work experience related to the required degree is required. The applicant must also have worked for at least one year after obtaining the degree.

Group D

This group includes occupations that require AQF Certificate IV. This group is divided into three categories:

  1. If the applicant's educational qualification and work experience are entirely relevant, the applicant must have a minimum of one year of work experience in the last five years after obtaining the degree.
  2. If the applicant's work experience is unrelated to their degree, the applicant must have a minimum of two years of work experience in the last five years after obtaining the degree.
  3. If the educational qualification is at the level of AQF Certificate III with work experience entirely relevant to the degree, the applicant must have a minimum of three years of work experience in the last five years after obtaining the degree.

Calculation of Pre-Qualification Work Experience: If all the work experience of the applicant before obtaining the AQF Certificate III or Certificate IV is equivalent, three years of work experience related to the degree before obtaining the degree is required. The applicant must also have a minimum of one year of work experience after obtaining the degree.

Group E

Includes occupations that require an AQF Diploma. The applicant must have a diploma that is completely relevant to the job, and during the last five years, the applicant must have a minimum of one year of work experience.

One of the advantages of credential assessment by this organization is the absence of the need for language proficiency documents. To have your documents assessed by VETASSESS Australia, applicants must select the relevant occupation code for their field and proceed accordingly. Choosing the most appropriate occupation code is of great importance. It has been observed that VETASSESS usually deducts one year from the provided work experience, even if all documents are accepted. This should be considered in presenting the intended work experience. The normal processing time for an assessment from VETASSESS is three to six months, and in the Fast Track option, it takes 10 to 20 days. The assessment fee for VETASSESS in 2023 is AUD 962 for regular processing and AUD 1585 for the Fast Track option. Note that extension of the assessment, or re-assessment, is not available in the Fast Track option.

Australia Engineers Assessment Organization (Engineers Australia) or EA

This organization assesses and evaluates engineering documents. The assessment of engineering qualifications by Engineers Australia (EA) might be slightly more challenging compared to the Australian Computer Society (ACS) and VETASSESS. In addition to general documents and work experience, Engineers Australia requires applicants to submit a Competency Demonstration Report (CDR). CDR consists of five parts, and in the first three parts, you need to present three projects as an engineer, such as a work project, a research paper, or even a thesis. It is advisable to present at least one or two work projects, each consisting of an average of 2000 to 2500 words, written in a specialized language and in the format required by Engineers Australia (EA). After presenting three projects, you must provide Continuing Professional Development (CPD), which includes courses, seminars, or any material you have studied outside the academic course and is in line with improving your skills and maintaining your profession. In the last section, you must write a Summary Statement, and the format and writing instructions are available on the Engineers Australia (EA) website. Engineers Australia (EA) also requires an IELTS Academic or General certificate with a score of 6 or a PTE score of 50 in each skill. The result of the assessment of applicants' qualifications by Engineers Australia and the equivalence of their qualifications will be in one of the following states. The final result of the equivalence of educational qualifications in Australia depends on factors such as the type of qualification, university, location of study, and field of study.

  • Assessment of educational qualifications equivalent to Professional Engineer: In this case, the applicant's field is equivalent to Skill Level 1. The assessment of professional engineers means an equivalent of a 4-year bachelor's degree in Australia. This educational qualification equivalence will give the applicant 15 points.
  • Assessment of educational qualifications equivalent to Engineering Technologist: In this case, the applicant's field is equivalent to Skill Level 2. The assessment of engineering technologists means an equivalent of a 3-year bachelor's degree in Australia. This educational qualification equivalence will give the applicant 10 points.
  • Assessment of educational qualifications equivalent to Engineering Associate: In this case, the applicant's field is equivalent to a 2-year diploma in Australia.
  • Assessment of educational qualifications equivalent to Engineering Manager: The result of this case's equivalence is for individuals with a senior management work experience. In this case, the applicant's field is equivalent to Skill Level 1. This educational qualification equivalence will give the applicant 15 points.

One noteworthy aspect based on applicants' experiences is that Engineers Australia (EA) usually does not deduct the number of years of applicants' work experience, and the work experience is calculated after obtaining the qualification. The response time of this organization is approximately 3 months, and the cost is 825 Australian dollars. However, if you choose to expedite the assessment and receive the response within 20 days, you need to pay an additional 325 Australian dollars on top of the original cost. In case of expiration and lapsing of the assessment by this organization, you don't need to resubmit all the documents. You only need to contact the organization for a re-assessment, and the cost is 125 Australian dollars. Usually, a response is provided within 10 days for a re-assessment.

Australian Trades Recognition Authority (TRA)

The Trades Recognition Australia (TRA) is responsible for assessing and recognizing the equivalence of over 130 technical trades in Australia. Applicants must initially submit their documents to TRA for credential evaluation and assessment. TRA employs two methods for credential recognition and assessment.

OSAP Method

For fields such as toolmaking, welding, cabinet making, carpentry, installation, construction, cooking, casting, high-pressure welding, welding supervisor, hairstyling, carpentry services, steam boiler making, metal casting with machinery, shaping sheet metal in the workshop, the OSAP method should be used for the equivalence of educational documents. In this method, there is no need for an English language certificate, but the interview is conducted in English, and the applicant is not allowed to use a translator.

Short courses or work experience or work registrations and licenses cannot replace the educational document. Also, the educational document must be approved by the Ministry of Science. Australian Computer Society (ACS) Document Assessment Organization

If you have graduated in one of the computer engineering fields or have work experience directly related to computer engineering, and you want to immigrate to Australia through a work visa, you need to send your documents to the Australian Computer Society (ACS). ACS is responsible for assessing the documents of individuals who have graduated in one of the computer science specialties or have work experience in the field of computer science and IT. ACS or the Australian Computer Society assesses the documents to be equivalent to Australian qualifications. Fortunately, ACS does not require English language proficiency as a prerequisite for document evaluation. As a result, engineers or graduates in computer science and IT can expedite their migration process. The Australian Computer Society (ACS) initially examines your academic documents based on the level of education and units completed at the university. Then, it categorizes them into one of the following groups: ICT Major, ICT Minor, or None-ICT.

  • In the ICT Major category, for postgraduate degrees, if your field aligns with your work experience, 2 years of experience are required, and if it's different, 4 years of work experience are needed. - In the ICT Minor category, with a bachelor's degree, depending on whether your qualification is relevant to your work experience or not, between 5 to 6 years of mandatory work experience is required. Microsoft and Cisco certifications usually fall into this category.
  • In the ICT Minor category with a bachelor's degree or higher, depending on the qualification and work experience, between 5 to 6 years of work experience is required.
  • If your qualification is a diploma or higher, and in the unit equivalence process, it does not fall into any of the above categories, you will be classified as None-ICT. For the assessment of your qualification by the ACS, you need 6 years of Australian work experience along with 2 RPL projects. (If your academic qualification is unrelated to computer science, at least 6 years of work experience along with RPL must be provided to ACS to receive your assessment. In this case, the entire work experience is deducted, and you won't have the chance to claim points in the EOI stage.)
  • Individuals seeking assessment by ACS with a diploma or lower must present 8 years of work experience along with 2 RPL projects.

In addition to all the mentioned requirements, it has been observed that ACS may deduct between 2 to 8 years from your work experience. Therefore, including the above points in presenting work experience and collecting documents seems essential. It is also advisable to upload documents such as salary slips for each year to ACS. Your employment certificate must be on letterhead, stamped, and signed. All documents, including contracts, should be stamped and signed by the highest authority in the organization or the HR manager. ACS assessment results are valid for up to 2 years. The cost of document assessment by this organization in 2023 is 530 Australian dollars.

Institute of Managers and Leaders Australia (IML)

The main responsibility of the Institute of Managers and Leaders (IML) is to conduct specialized training courses in various fields, including financial matters, marketing, sales, communications, and management. In addition, the organization plays a crucial role in assessing the professional skills of individuals in various managerial positions for those seeking work migration to Australia, whose work documents need evaluation in the country. It's worth mentioning that managers approved by IML are those engaged in large organizations with multilayered management hierarchies and a relevant number of subordinates, and these documents should be presented through formal organizational charts.

Evaluation Criteria:

The IML organization serves as the reference for skill assessment by the Australian government, particularly in evaluating high-level management skills. The assessment criteria are based on the level of work experience, training, and acquired competencies. Proof of insurance is required for this organization.

Required Documents for Assessment at the Institute of Managers and Leaders Australia (IML)

  • Identification documents and educational certificates, including transcripts.
  • Work-related documents, including a letter of employment history, job descriptions, employment contracts, and salary slips.
  • Organizational charts detailing all departments, managerial hierarchies, and subordinate personnel.
  • Valid recommendation letters from other managers working in the same company.
  • A reputable website, preferably searchable in English.

Please note that this organization does not require an English language proficiency document. It examines work experience over the last 8 to 10 years (after the graduation date). In this assessment, the relevance of the educational document to work experience is not mandatory. The validity period of this assessment is 3 years from the date of issue.
